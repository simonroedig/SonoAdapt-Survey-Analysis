# SonoAdapt Online Survey Compressed to Save Tokens

## 1. Introduction, Consent, & Setup

* **Context:** Study on how smart glasses can intelligently adapt audio notifications based on the user's environment.


* **Requirements:** Desktop/laptop only, headphones/earbuds required, good English proficiency.


* **Consent:** Standard GDPR compliance, anonymous data collection, voluntary participation.


* **Tech Checks:** Verification of headphone use, audio volume adjustment, and left/right/both channel audio check.



---

## 2. Demographics & Background

* **Basic Demographics:** Age, Gender, Native Language, English proficiency (1-7 scale), and Hearing impairment status.


* **Notification Habits:** Current standard smartphone notification setting (mute, visual, vibrate, audio combinations) and the primary reason for this setting (Social, Focus, Privacy, Awareness, Habit).


* **Smart Glasses Experience:** Frequency of prior smart (AI) glasses usage.


* **Notification Preferences:** Types of notifications users would want auditorily via smart glasses (Calls, Messages, Emails, Calendar, Navigation, etc.).


* **Importance/Urgency:** Agreement scales (1-7) on whether users only want auditory notifications for highly important or highly urgent messages.



---

## 3. Familiarization Phase

* **The Stimulus:** Users are introduced to an urgent and important WhatsApp message from a close friend named Lilly: "When are you here?".


* **The Versions:** Users listen to 3 distinct audio notification versions delivered privately via smart glasses speakers.


* **Comprehension & Preference:** Users identify which versions stated the sender's name and choose their general preferred version (or none) with a text explanation.


* **Speech Acceptability:** 1-7 agreement scale on assuming speech output means the message is urgent/important, and general acceptability of unprompted speech notifications.


* **Follow-ups:** Factors influencing speech acceptability (Privacy, Understanding, Intrusiveness, Context, etc.), acceptable maximum length of spoken notifications, and acceptance of automatic summarization for long messages.



---

## 4. The 9 Contextual Scenarios

**The Situations (A-I):**

* **Scenario A:** Home Alone Computer – Writing code, solving a complex problem.


* **Scenario B:** Team Meeting – Lively office meeting, continuous background speech.


* **Scenario C:** Home Music – Relaxing alone with loud stereo music playing.


* **Scenario D:** Tent – Physically setting up a tent outdoors with friends and music.


* **Scenario E:** Cycle City – Cycling alone through a quiet city street with minor traffic.


* **Scenario F:** Grocery Shopping – In a supermarket with occupied hands, background noise, and announcements.


* **Scenario G:** Cooking Dinner – Home alone cooking while listening to a podcast.


* **Scenario H:** Study Coffee Shop – Studying alone with loud background music and people around.


* **Scenario I:** Quiet Friend Over – Lively conversation with a friend who temporarily checks her phone.



**Standard Question Block (Asked for EVERY Scenario A-I):**

* **Appropriateness:** Rate Version 1, 2, and 3 (1-7 scale from Completely inappropriate to Completely appropriate).


* **Detectability:** Rate Version 1, 2, and 3 (1-7 scale from Very difficult to Very easy to detect).


* **Disruption:** Rate Version 1, 2, and 3 (1-7 scale from Not disruptive at all to Extremely disruptive).


* **Social Acceptability:** Rate Version 1, 2, and 3 (1-7 scale from Completely unacceptable to Completely acceptable).


* **Overall Preference:** Choose Version 1, 2, 3, or None, followed by a free-text explanation.


* **Influencing Factors:** Select reasons for preference (Acoustic, Focus, Social, Safety, Info, Other).


* **Timing Preference:** Choose when the audio should play (Immediately, at a natural pause/break, after task completion, or later).


* **Delay Fallback:** If delayed timing was chosen, select which notification version should play when that time arrives, followed by a free-text explanation.



(Note: Two standard Attention Checks asking the user to select a specific Likert scale option are interspersed between scenarios to verify data quality.)

---

## 5. Final Evaluation Matrices

* **Preference Matrix:** Users select their preferred audio notification style (Mute, Sound Only, Speech Sender, Speech Sender+Content) across four different message types: Simple acknowledgment, Routine update, Great non-urgent news, and Time-sensitive/action-required situations.


* **Delay Acceptability Matrix:** Users define the maximum acceptable delay (No delay, Brief delay/seconds, Moderate delay/minutes, Indefinite delay/hours) for the same four message types listed above.


* **Final Comments:** Optional open-text field for additional thoughts or impressions.
